Trading Account and Demat Concepts:

1) Buyer has  Rs 1,00,000 in his bank account say. Nabil Bank

2)He has a trading account and wants to transfer Rs 50000 to his trading account.

3) If he has transferred to the trading account, remaining amount he has in Bank Account: Rs 50,000

4) Has Rs50,000 in his trading account.

5) He wants to purchase some shares (sat HDFC) in the secondary market.

6) Remaining money he would have in trading account: Rs. 30000

7) If he wants to buy then he should use Trading Account Dashboard, he puts order for 20 HDFC shares at Rs 1000 each and found someone (seller) is selling 20 share.

8) If someone sells then Rs20,000 get deposited to Seller Trading Account.

9) Seller 20 shares are deducted from his Demat account

10) NSE/BSE hold it.

11) then after 2 days, 20 shares are transferred to the buyer's account.

12) Buyer is getting 20 shares after two days in his Demat

13) If the seller wants to send 10,000 from the trading account to Saving Account then he can do it.

14) 10,000 gets added to Saving Account and 10000 is removed from Trading Account.

Note that : Shares number (here 20) are stored in DEMAT Account. and Money(here 20,000) related information are stored in Trading Account
