Saving Account

Recurring Deposit(RD)

  • Deposit a fixed amount of money every month/every day upto fixed period of time ex. 6 month, 1 yr, 5 yr etc and cannot withdraw in between.
  • Provides more interest 9 to 12 percent.
  • We have seen a person come to the shop and ask  Rs. 100 and entry in the book each day. This is a Recurring Deposit.
  • Ranges from 7 days to 10 years

Fixed Deposit(FD)

  • Deposit fixed amount only one time and cannot withdraw until the fixed time is reached.
  • Provides large Interest generally 7 to 9 %
  • Ranges from 6 month to 10 years

DEMAT Account (DA ==> Dematerialization Account)

Many years ago, when you bought company shares then the company provides a physical share certificate.So, this is old paper work method. DEMAT is modern method of doing the same things, but in digital form. This transforms the share market from physical form to digital form. 

 Why you REALLY need a Demat account?

  1. You cannot buy or sell stocks without a demat account 
  2. You cannot invest in equity directly without it
  3. Mandated by SEBI for transactions of listed company securities

So when you place an order to buy a stock, the shares get credited (comes in) to your Demat account. Similarly, when you choose to sell your shares, they get debited from your Demat account.

 

The benefit of DEMAT Account

1. Security of shares: The most important benefit of Demat account is that it is safer than holding physical shares which can get lost, damaged, or stolen.

2. No forgery or theft Since the Demat account is electronic in nature, the risk of documents getting stolen, damaged, or lost does not exist.

3. Seamless trading and transfer Unlike transporting physical certificates, Demat accounts allow the transfer of shares quickly and securely. This has lowered order processing times.

4. Lower cost Physical share certificates attract paperwork and stamp duty, which increases costs. With a Demat account, all this is eradicated and you can get a Demat account in no time.

5. Multiple access points A Demat account is operated electronically, which essentially means that users can access the account from multiple touch points—mobile, tablet, PC, laptop, etc.
