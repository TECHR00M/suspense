Life Insurance

  • Insurance against the risk associated with a person's life.
  • Provide financial protection against the disaster life of those have taken insurance.

Agreement Between Parties

Insurer: Insurance Company.

Insured: The person who takes insurance policy

Agent: Middleman or representative.

  • In order to ensure life, an insurance contract must be signed with a life insurance company. Contract is usually for a minimum of 5 years.
  • The premium rate is determined based on the age of insured, sum insured, health condition, etc.
  • If you insured at older age , then premium will be higher.
  • It can be paid annually ,quaterly , monthly as per the wish of the insured.

Term Life Insurance

  • The term means you will get insurance till a certain age not for your entire life.
  • It is a kind of life insurance that is for a defined period of time.
  • Here, a nominee of the insured receives the sum if the insured person dies during the specified term.
  • Here, if the insured donot dies on specified term time, then money will collapsed.
  • The premium is very low.
  • Start this insurance at early age .Less age more less premium value.

Suppose at age 25, i am taking Term Life Insurance for next 35 years (ie 35 + 25 = upto 60 year old), with premium 5000 yearly. So, have I have decided to pay 5000 each year for the next 35 years.

Suppose I died at 50 then my family member will get a much more sum of money from insurance company. If I do not die at 60 then you will not pay the premium and you will get nothing from life insurance company.

 

Endowment LIfe Insurance (Traditional Insurance plan popular in nepal)

It is the insurance in which the insured (if he/she is live ) or nominee (if the insured dies) receives the sizable insured sum along with bonus after the expiration of the policy system.

Here, the premium is normally high than Term Life Insurance., Risk is also less so return from company is also less.

Non-Life Insurance

  • Includes General insurance(Moter Insurance,Fire Insurance, Reinsurance,Commerical Insurance,Pet Insurance etc), property insurance, and casualty insurance are other names of non-life insurance.
