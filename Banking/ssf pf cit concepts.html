Employee vs Employer

  • The employer is a person, firm, or other entity which pays for or hires the services of another person.
  • The employee is an individual who provides labor to a company or another person.

SSF

  • Stands for Social Security Fund(सामाजिक सुरक्षा कोष).
  • It has already come into force from Shrawan 2076
  • It is decided that a total of 31% of the basic salary of employees is required to be contributed to SSF. 20% by the employer and 11% by the employee. 
  1. How can one participate in SSF?
    By visiting the website of SSF at www.ssf.gov.np one can register by clicking SOSYS online Registration after which a unique SSF number, username and password can be obtained. After this all the necessary details of employer and employee can be updated and uploaded online.

     

  2. What benefits can one obtain after participating in SSF?
    -Medical checkups and maternity security plan
    -Accidental and physically impaired Security plan
    -Dependent family security plan
    -Old age security plan

  3. How to deposit amount in SSF?
    Amounts can be deposited online in SSF through Connect IPS. SSF bears a call account in Nepal Bank Limited, having account number 00211606720012000002.

  4. Is it absolutely compulsory to participate in SSF?
    It is a compulsory legal requirement provisioned by Labour Act to participate in SSF.

  5. What is the total contribution to be made in SSF?
    31% of the basic salary of an employee has to be deposited in the SSF. 20% of which shall be borne by the employer and 11% shall be borne by the employee.

  6. What happens if an employee resigns from an enterprise and joins another enterprise?
    The social security number of such employee shall remain the same. Regular contribution shall have to be made even from a different entity.

  7. What if someone quits a job and starts own business?
    Self-employed persons can also be a part of SSF by depositing regular contribution. In case if someone quits an existing job and starts own business, such person can continue to contribute in SSF.
  8. Is it necessary to deposit 1% social security tax after participating in SSF?
    No. 1% social security tax is not required to be paid after contributing in SSF.

  9. Until what age does one have to contribute in SSF?
    Until 60 years of age. One has to contribute for 15 years regularly to get pension from SSF. If someone retires before contributing for 15 years, such employee shall receive lump sum.

Fixed Deposit:

  • Low Risk
  • High Liquidity ( you can withdraw money any time)
  • Interest are taxable. (Tax is applied to interest)

Provident Fund

  • Low Risk because government control
  • Low liquidity
  • Interest are non taxable (Tax is not applied to interest)
  • High Interest Rate ( 8 - 9%)

Two types : 

1) Public Provident Fund (PPF): Any one can open PPF. Can withdraw money after 15 year of deposit.Partial Withdraw in emergency case.

2) Employee Provident Fund( EPF): If you are employers of certain company.Then company will open EPF . Can withdraw money after you left your job. Partial Withdraw in emergency case.

When you change company then new company will generate new PF for you. And amount you have in your previous

PF has to transfer to new PF.
